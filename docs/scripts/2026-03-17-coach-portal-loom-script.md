# Coach Portal Demo — Loom Video Script

**Speaker:** Amit Bhatia, development partner
**Audience:** FranklinCovey executive coaches
**Target length:** ~4 minutes
**Date:** March 17, 2026

---

## Opening (0:00–0:20)

[SCREEN: Browser on the sign-in page]

Hi, I'm Amit. I built the coaching platform you'll be using to log your sessions with USPS leadership participants. I know your time is valuable, so I'm going to keep this short and walk you through exactly what you need to know to get started. The whole session-logging process takes about two minutes once you're in.

---

## Sign In (0:20–0:55)

[SCREEN: Sign-in page with email field visible]

Let's start with sign-in. There's no password to remember. You enter your email address, click "Send sign-in link," and check your inbox.

[SCREEN: Click "Send sign-in link," then switch to email inbox showing the magic link email]

You'll get an email with a "Sign in" button. Click it, and you're in. That's the whole process. If you close your browser and come back later, you'll request a new link the same way.

[PAUSE — let the sign-in complete and the dashboard load]

---

## Dashboard (0:55–1:35)

[SCREEN: Coach dashboard, fully loaded — stat cards and engagement list visible]

This is your dashboard. It's the first thing you see when you sign in. At the top, you've got three numbers: your active engagements, sessions logged this week, and your completion rate. Quick snapshot of where things stand.

[SCREEN: Scroll down to the "Needs attention" section]

Below that is a "Needs attention" section. If any of your engagements are overdue for a session, they'll show up here so you can act on them right away.

[SCREEN: Scroll to the "My engagements" list with Active and Completed tabs]

And then you've got your full engagement list. You can toggle between active and completed. Each row shows the participant, their program, how many sessions you've logged out of the total, and the current status. To open an engagement, you click the row.

---

## Engagement Detail (1:35–2:05)

[SCREEN: Click into an active engagement — engagement detail page loads]

Here's what an individual engagement looks like. At the top, you've got the participant's email, their program and cohort, a status badge, and progress — in this case, zero of two sessions logged.

[SCREEN: Point out the "Book next session" button in the upper right]

You'll also see a "Book next session" link up here. That opens your Calendly page so the participant can schedule with you, or you can share it with them directly.

---

## Session Logging Form (2:05–3:20)

[SCREEN: The "Log session" tab is active, showing the session logging form]

This is where you'll spend most of your time, and it's designed to be fast. The form has three sections.

[SCREEN: Scroll through "Session basics" — status dropdown, date picker, topic dropdown]

First, session basics. You pick the session status — completed or forfeited — set the date, and choose the topic from a dropdown. The topics are specific to the program, so you'll see the right ones for MLP, ALP, EF, or EL.

[SCREEN: Scroll to "Session assessment" — outcomes checkboxes, next steps, engagement level, action-commitment]

Second, session assessment. You check off your session outcomes — things like "Action plan created" or "Participant committed to an action." You select next steps, rate the participant's engagement level on a one-to-five scale, and note whether they followed through on commitments from the last session. The engagement scale shows you descriptions at one, three, and five to anchor your rating, so you're not guessing.

[SCREEN: Scroll to "Notes" section and the auto-save indicator]

Third, notes. Anything you want to capture here is visible to program administrators, so keep that in mind. Once you've filled everything out, hit "Log session" and you're done.

[SCREEN: Click "Log session" — form submits, session appears in timeline]

[PAUSE — let the session save and the confirmation appear]

Now here's the part I think you'll appreciate. Once a session is saved, any edits you make auto-save. You'll see "All changes saved" at the bottom of the form. No submit button to hunt for, no worrying about losing your work.

---

## Session History and Editing (3:20–3:45)

[SCREEN: Click the "Session history" tab — timeline of logged sessions visible]

Over on the session history tab, you can see every session you've logged for this participant in a timeline. If you need to update something — maybe you want to add a note after the fact — click "Edit" on any session and the form repopulates with that session's data. Make your changes, and they auto-save.

[PAUSE — show the edit button and form repopulating]

---

## Completed Engagement (3:45–4:00)

[SCREEN: Navigate to a completed engagement — completion banner visible, no log form]

When all sessions are logged for an engagement, the logging form goes away and you see a completion banner along with the full session history. Clean and clear — nothing left to do.

---

## Close (4:00–4:15)

[SCREEN: Back on the dashboard]

That's it. Sign in with a magic link, check your dashboard, click into an engagement, log your session in about two minutes, and move on with your day.

If you run into anything or have questions, reach out to Andrea Sherman at andrea.sherman@franklincovey.com. She'll loop me in if it's a technical issue.

Thanks for your time, and thanks for the work you do with these leaders.
